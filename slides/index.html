International NodeBots Day 2014

Melbourne

Hi everyone. Welcome to International NodeBots Day here in Melbourne

A year ago this weekend, there were a group of about 30 of us in a poky office in Sydney where we managed to cobble together some nodebots. This was part of last year's international nodebots day where there were another handful of locations doing the same thing as well

No one really thought much more of it other than it was a bit of fun, though those involved kept building things.

NBD Melbourne, 2013

But then I started getting questions about doing another one but in Melbourne, and the same from peeps in Brisbane

NBD Brisbane, 2013

All of a sudden three cities in Aus had had them and we were asked to do workshops at CampJS as well as sessions at hacker spaces informally, and people in other cities want to host them too

International NodeBots Day

Which brings us to today. Here in Australia we are kicking off NodeBots Day along with Sydney and Brisbane at the same time. We'll have a google hangout with them later so we can collectively share what we're doing

Over the course of tonight and tomorrow for us, at least 25 other locations around the world will all be doing the same sorts of things - playing, learning and building JS based physical things.

Play, Learn, Build

Over the last 12 months, one of the key themes to emerge has been that people like you - designers, developers and others interested in this space - are keen to use JavaScript to work with physical technologies.

Sure, JS has its limitations - it's hard to run it at the level of a chip for example. But for experimentation, for learning fundamental electronics and just playing with an idea to work out if it's even possible JS is approachable, well documented and has a big developer community.

If JS is a gateway for more people building hardware then I'm all for it, even when I flip between C and JS interchangeably these days.
